The Fracture Method

A practical system
for testing arguments.

Fracture looks past grammar and asks the questions a coach, teacher, judge, or opponent would ask: what is the claim, what proves it, what connects the proof to the claim, and what breaks under pressure?

What Fracture Checks

The parts of an argument that decide whether it survives.

Thesis and Claim

Fracture identifies what the writer is actually trying to prove and whether the claim is specific, arguable, and narrow enough to defend.

Evidence and Warrant

The report separates the supporting fact from the reasoning bridge. A source can be real and still fail to prove the claim if the warrant is weak.

Assumptions and Counterarguments

Fracture finds hidden assumptions, predicts the strongest fair objections, and turns those objections into revision tasks and crossfire questions.

Rhetoric and Audience

The tool checks whether the argument is clear, speakable, persuasive, and suited to the reader, teacher, judge, or audience it needs to reach.

Revision Priority

Instead of treating every comment as equal, Fracture ranks the repairs that most improve the argument: main claim, warrant, rebuttal, source, and conclusion.

Access

The method is built for students who may not have regular private coaching. It gives a serious first-pass review that is easier to understand and act on.

The Problem

Surface-level feedback
leaves gaps hidden.

Writers need more than broad reassurance. A useful review should quote the exact sentence, explain the reasoning problem, and show what a stronger version could look like.

Real argument analysis requires fallacy awareness, source judgment, assumption testing, and the ability to model strong counterarguments fairly.

Fracture is built to make revision decisions easier: identify the most important weakness, explain why it matters, and give the writer a practical way forward.

Surface-Level Response

"Your essay presents a well-structured argument with clear supporting evidence. The thesis is stated clearly, and the body paragraphs logically flow from one to the next. Consider strengthening your conclusion by revisiting your main points."

- No quotes. No specifics. No clear revision priority.
Fracture Response

Claim rated WEAK. Quote: "studies consistently show..."

Diagnosis: Weasel quantifier. "Studies" is unattributed and permits any quantity. A single contradicting study weakens this claim.

Opponent exploit: "Which studies? When? What methodology?"

+ Text-specific. Verifiable. Ready to revise.
