---
title: "What the AI Will Not Do"
description: "The guardrails that keep Fracture's feedback honest."
---

The Fracture Engine is built to be useful without being misleading. These are hard rules it follows in every report.

## It will not invent evidence

The engine never fabricates statistics, years, study findings, examples, organizations, quotations, sources, or links to make a rewrite sound more persuasive. When a stronger version would require evidence your draft does not supply, it marks the exact spot with `[verified evidence needed]` instead of inventing a fact.

## It will not pretend a source is verified

It never claims a citation has been checked when it has not. When a factual statement needs checking, it says exactly what to verify and keeps evaluating the logic.

## It will not fake support for attacks

The same rule applies to counterarguments and the attack tree. The engine will not claim that studies, institutions, or outcomes exist unless your draft supplied them. It describes the kind of evidence an opponent would need rather than pretending it has been found.

## It will not pad the report

Sections contain as many distinct, useful points as your draft supports — not a fixed quota of filler. The same weakness is not repeated across multiple sections in slightly different words.

<Note>
These guardrails are why a low source score does not crush your reasoning score. The engine keeps "what is proven" and "what still needs proof" clearly separate.
</Note>
