---

After the reasoning analysis, the Fracture Engine runs a separate source pass. It pulls the factual claims out of your draft and searches the public web to see what support exists.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Claim extraction">
    The engine pulls out quoted passages, the claims it flagged during analysis, and factual-looking sentences from your draft.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Public web search">
    For each claim, it searches public pages and retrieves page text to compare against your claim.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Match scoring">
    It compares the retrieved page against your claim and assigns a support status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Citation building">
    For well-matched claims, it builds a starter MLA or APA entry from the page's details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Support statuses

- **Likely supported** — a retrieved page closely matches the claim.
- **Partial match** — part of the claim matches, but not every detail is confirmed.
- **Needs review** — pages were found but do not clearly support the claim.
- **Citation incomplete** — the draft does not name the source precisely enough.
- **Source not found** — no usable public result was returned.

<Warning>
Every match is tentative. Open the linked page and confirm the exact passage before using it in a final paper or speech.
</Warning>
