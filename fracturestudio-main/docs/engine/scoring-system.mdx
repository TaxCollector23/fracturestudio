---
title: "Scoring System"
description: "How the Fracture Engine turns an analysis into a score."
---

Every audit produces an overall score from 0 to 100, plus a breakdown across the parts of an argument that decide whether it survives.

## The overall score

| Range | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 90–100 | Resilient under close questioning |
| 75–89 | Strong with fixable pressure points |
| 60–74 | Usable but vulnerable |
| 40–59 | Major revision needed |
| 11–39 | The argument breaks under pressure |
| 0–10 | Not enough of an argument to evaluate |

## The breakdown

The score separates distinct dimensions so you can see which area needs attention first rather than relying on a single number. Depending on the mode, these include argument strength, assumption safety, logic, and rhetoric.

## How it is calibrated

- A clear, coherent, well-warranted draft can score in the 80s or 90s even if its citations still need work.
- The engine does not push a draft into the 20s or 30s simply because sources still need verification.
- Very low scores are reserved for drafts that are not meaningful arguments, are mostly unsupported assertion, or collapse in their central reasoning.

<Note>
Citation completeness is treated as a verification task, not as automatic proof that an argument is weak. Reasoning is scored first.
</Note>
