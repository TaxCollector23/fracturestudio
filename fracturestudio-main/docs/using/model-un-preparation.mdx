Use Fracture to prepare for Model UN by running your position paper through Argument mode and then building rebuttals.

## Prepare a position paper

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run your position paper in Argument mode">
    Fracture maps your country's stance, the claims behind it, and the warrants that connect your policy to the problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the weak links">
    Look at the collapse point and assumption audit to see where another delegate or the chair will push hardest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build your rebuttals">
    Use the rebuttal builder to anticipate objections from blocs with competing interests and to prepare responses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to focus on

- Whether your proposed policy actually solves the problem you describe
- Where your stance needs a clearer warrant or a stronger source
- The strongest competing solutions other delegates will propose, and how to answer them
- Crossfire-style questions you can raise during debate

<Tip>
Run your draft resolution clauses through Fracture the same way. Each operative clause is a claim that needs a warrant.
</Tip>
