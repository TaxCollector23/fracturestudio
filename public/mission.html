Our Mission

Arguments deserve
real criticism.

The world has enough writing assistants that tell you your work is "generally well-structured." We built Fracture to do the opposite: find every flaw, name it precisely, and show you exactly how to close it.

The Problem

Generic feedback is
a failure mode.

When ChatGPT tells you your essay has "strong supporting evidence" without quoting a single sentence, it isn't helping you — it's producing the appearance of analysis without the substance of it.

Real argument analysis is hard. It requires knowing the names of fallacies, understanding which assumptions bear the most argumentative load, and being able to construct the strongest possible counterargument — not a strawman.

We built Fracture because we were tired of AI tools that validate instead of challenge. Real strength comes from stress-testing, not from praise.

Generic AI Response

"Your essay presents a well-structured argument with clear supporting evidence. The thesis is stated clearly, and the body paragraphs logically flow from one to the next. Consider strengthening your conclusion by revisiting your main points."

— No quotes. No specifics. Could apply to any essay ever written.
Fracture Response

Claim rated WEAK. Quote: "studies consistently show..."

Diagnosis: Weasel quantifier — "studies" is unattributed and permits any quantity. A single contradicting study defeats this claim.

Opponent exploit: "Which studies? When? What methodology?"

+ Essay-specific. Falsifiable. Actionable.
Our Approach

The standards your analysis is held to.

Every finding is tied to your exact words.

Every flaw we surface, every fallacy we name, every assumption we expose is tied to the exact sentence in your essay where it lives. You always know precisely what to fix and where — not a vague suggestion floating without context.

Feedback you can actually act on.

Vague feedback is useless. Every diagnosis Fracture delivers is specific enough to be argued with — and specific enough to fix. You will always know exactly what is wrong, why it matters, and what a better version looks like. Nothing is left to interpretation.

Your opposition is built to defeat you.

We construct the single strongest version of the opposing view — written as a genuine believer of that position would argue it — and then show you exactly which of your claims it threatens and how to preempt it before anyone can use it against you.

We write the better version, not just describe it.

When Fracture identifies a weak sentence, it replaces it. Every rewrite suggestion is a complete, ready-to-use sentence you can drop directly into your document, adapt, or learn from. You get the improved version — not instructions on how to find it yourself.

Scoring that shows you exactly where to improve.

A single overall score tells you little. Fracture breaks every audit into four independent dimensions — Argument Strength, Assumption Audit, Logic, and Rhetoric — each scored out of 25. Know exactly which area is holding you back, track it across drafts, and close the gaps that matter most.

What We Analyze

Four dimensions.
Complete picture.

Argument Strength — Thesis clarity, claim ratings (STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK), logical flaw diagnosis, opponent exploits, and concrete fixes for each claim.

Assumption Audit — Every implicit premise is surfaced, rated by load-bearing weight (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW), and given a vulnerability assessment plus a defense strategy.

Logic & Fallacies — Named fallacies only, quoted verbatim, explained in context, and given complete rewrites. No vague "this could be clearer."

Rhetoric — Opening hook, paragraph flow, your single strongest and weakest sentence identified verbatim, with a complete rewrite for the weakest.
