The Problem

Surface-level feedback
leaves gaps hidden.

Writers need more than broad reassurance. A useful review should quote the exact sentence, explain the reasoning problem, and show what a stronger version could look like.

Real argument analysis requires fallacy awareness, source judgment, assumption testing, and the ability to model strong counterarguments fairly.

Fracture is built to make revision decisions easier: identify the most important weakness, explain why it matters, and give the writer a practical way forward.

Surface-Level Response

"Your essay presents a well-structured argument with clear supporting evidence. The thesis is stated clearly, and the body paragraphs logically flow from one to the next. Consider strengthening your conclusion by revisiting your main points."

- No quotes. No specifics. No clear revision priority.
Fracture Response

Claim rated WEAK. Quote: "studies consistently show..."

Diagnosis: Weasel quantifier. "Studies" is unattributed and permits any quantity. A single contradicting study weakens this claim.

Opponent exploit: "Which studies? When? What methodology?"

+ Text-specific. Verifiable. Ready to revise.
Our Approach

The standards your analysis is held to.

Every finding is tied to your exact words.

Every issue we surface, every fallacy we name, and every assumption we expose is tied to the exact sentence where it lives. You always know precisely what to fix and where.

Feedback you can actually act on.

Every diagnosis Fracture delivers is specific enough to evaluate and specific enough to revise from. You see what is weak, why it matters, and what a better version looks like.

Opposing views are represented fairly.

Fracture constructs the strongest reasonable opposing view and then shows which claims it challenges, where a rebuttal is needed, and how to answer with evidence or a narrower claim.

We write the better version, not just describe it.

When Fracture identifies a weak sentence, it provides a complete replacement option you can adopt, adapt, or study. The goal is to make the next draft easier to write.

Scoring that shows you exactly where to improve.

A single overall score tells only part of the story. Fracture separates argument strength, assumption safety, logic, and rhetoric so writers can see which area needs attention first.

What We Analyze

Four dimensions.
Complete picture.

Argument Strength - Thesis clarity, claim ratings (STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK), logical flaw diagnosis, reader challenges, and concrete fixes for each claim.

Assumption Audit - Every implicit premise is surfaced, rated by load-bearing weight (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW), and given a vulnerability assessment plus a defense strategy.

Logic & Fallacies - Named fallacies only, quoted verbatim, explained in context, and given complete rewrites. No vague "this could be clearer."

Rhetoric - Opening hook, paragraph flow, your single strongest and weakest sentence identified verbatim, with a complete rewrite for the weakest.
