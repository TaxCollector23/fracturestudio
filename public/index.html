Why Fracture

Feedback built for
serious revision.

Fracture turns a draft into a structured diagnosis: what the argument claims, what supports it, what is assumed, and what should be fixed first.

Standard Writing Feedback
Broad comments without anchors. Helpful encouragement has limits when it does not point to the exact sentence that needs attention.
Reusable advice instead of text-specific diagnosis. General tips can be useful, but revision moves become stronger when they respond to your actual wording.
Limited pressure testing. Many tools review clarity but do not model how a skeptical reader, judge, or opponent would question the claim.
Hidden assumptions stay hidden. The most important premises are often unstated, which makes them easy to miss during revision.
No clear revision priority. Without a ranked path, writers often spend time polishing sentences while the main argument still needs support.
Fracture Studio
Verbatim quotes from your actual draft. Every diagnosis is anchored to the sentence it evaluates, so the next edit is easy to find.
Text-specific, testable diagnoses. Each observation explains what is weak, why it matters, and how to improve it.
Strong counterarguments before they surprise you. Fracture models the best response against your position and shows which claims need protection.
Assumption review with load-bearing ratings. Hidden premises are surfaced, rated HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW, and paired with a defense strategy.
Four-part scoring that explains the result. Argument strength, assumption safety, logic, and rhetoric are scored separately so improvement is measurable.
Full Feature Breakdown

Eight lenses.
One complete audit.

Every analysis produces a structured report across the parts of argument quality that matter in academic and debate settings. The goal is not more comments. The goal is a better next draft.

4-Dimensional Scoring

A 100-point composite score broken into Argument Strength, Assumption Audit, Logic, and Rhetoric, each out of 25. See where the draft is strongest and where points are being lost.

Claim-by-Claim Review

Every body claim is rated STRONG, MODERATE, or WEAK. Each gets a named issue, a precise diagnosis, the challenge a skeptical reader would raise, and a concrete fix.

Hidden Assumption Audit

Surfaces premises the draft relies on but does not yet defend. Each assumption is rated by load-bearing weight, with guidance on what evidence or reasoning would support it.

Logical Fallacy Detection

Named fallacies only. Each issue is identified by exact name, quoted verbatim, explained in context, and paired with a more defensible version.

Steelmanned Counter-Arguments

Fracture builds the strongest reasonable opposing argument, then shows which specific claims it challenges and how to answer it with stronger evidence or a narrower claim.

Rhetorical Analysis

Evaluates opening hook, paragraph flow, and the single strongest and weakest sentence verbatim. The weakest sentence includes a complete rewrite option.

Rewrite Suggestions

Full sentence rewrites for high-impact improvement opportunities. Each rewrite shows what a clearer claim, warrant, or transition can look like.

Export, Share & Save

Export the structured report data, create a shareable link, and save drafts locally for later revision.
