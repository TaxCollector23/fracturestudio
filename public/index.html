Why Fracture

Feedback built for
serious revision.

Fracture turns a draft into a structured diagnosis: what the argument claims, what supports it, what is assumed, and what should be fixed first.

Typical Review
Comments float above the page. The writer receives advice but still has to hunt for the sentence that caused the problem.
Advice sounds interchangeable. Notes about clarity or support rarely explain why this particular draft becomes vulnerable.
The strongest objection is missing. A draft can read smoothly while its central premise remains easy to challenge.
Unstated premises go untouched. The argument depends on ideas that never become visible enough to defend.
Every note appears equally urgent. Surface polish can take time away from the few changes that would materially strengthen the draft.
Fracture Studio
Each note begins with the source line. The report leads directly back to the wording that needs attention.
Each weakness is explained in context. You see the failure mode, the consequence, and the repair strategy together.
The draft faces a credible opposing case. Pressure testing reveals which parts need evidence, qualification, or a narrower promise.
Implicit premises become reviewable. Hidden dependencies are surfaced and ranked by how much weight they carry.
The report ends with an order of operations. You leave with a short sequence of edits rather than a pile of disconnected comments.
Inside the Review

One pass. Eight ways
to interrogate a draft.

The report separates reasoning, evidence, rhetoric, and practical revision work so you can move from inspection to editing without losing the thread.

Scorecard

A compact view of argument strength, unstated premises, logic, and rhetorical execution.

Claim Inventory

A line-by-line account of the positions your draft actually commits to defending.

Premise Scan

A ranked list of ideas the argument relies on without stating or protecting them.

Logic Check

Named reasoning errors tied to the exact line where the argument overreaches.

Opposition Model

A serious counter-position designed to reveal the weakest parts of your case.

Delivery Review

A reading of the opening, paragraph movement, strongest line, and weakest line.

Repair Drafts

Replacement language for high-impact claims, warrants, transitions, and conclusions.

Working Record

Save the draft, export the report, copy structured data, or share the review link.
