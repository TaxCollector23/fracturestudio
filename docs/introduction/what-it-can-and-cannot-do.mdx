title: "What it can and cannot do"
description: "An honest picture of where Fracture helps and where it stops."
---

Fracture Studio is a reasoning tool, not a fact oracle. Knowing its limits makes its feedback more useful.

## What it can do

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Analyze reasoning">
    Map claims, warrants, evidence, and impacts, and find where the logic breaks.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Surface assumptions">
    Name the unstated ideas your argument depends on and rate how load-bearing each one is.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Predict attacks">
    Build the strongest fair counterarguments and the questions an opponent would ask.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Check sources">
    Search the public web for your factual claims and flag what still needs a citation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Rewrite weak lines">
    Provide complete replacement sentences you can adopt, adapt, or study.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Prioritize fixes">
    Rank repairs so you know what to change first.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What it cannot do

- **It does not guarantee facts.** The engine never pretends a source has been verified. It tells you what to check.
- **It does not invent evidence.** It will not fabricate statistics, studies, quotations, or sources to make a rewrite sound stronger. Where evidence is needed, it marks the gap instead.
- **It does not replace your judgment.** A source match is a starting point. Open it and confirm the exact passage before relying on it.
- **It does not write your argument for you.** It improves the draft you bring; it is a sparring partner, not a ghostwriter.

<Warning>
Always open any linked source and confirm it before citing it in a final paper or speech. Fracture surfaces candidates; you confirm them.
</Warning>
