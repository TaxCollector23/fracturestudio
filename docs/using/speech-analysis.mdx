---
title: "Speech Analysis"
description: "Grade a piece the way an audience hears it once."
---

Speech mode grades your writing as a live spoken piece for an audience, not as a debate flow or a school essay.

## What it prioritizes

- Hook and opening
- Audience clarity and the persuasive arc
- Organization, signposting, and transitions
- Pacing, pauses, and emphasis
- Credibility and the placement of stories and examples
- Memorable lines and a clear call to action

Opponent attacks are secondary here. Listener doubts, confusion, boredom, and credibility gaps are primary.

## The Audience Friction Point

In speech mode, the report's load-bearing field becomes the **Audience Friction Point** — the moment a listener is most likely to lose the thread — instead of a debate-style collapse point.

<Tip>
Speech mode tells you where to pause, what to emphasize, which transition to make clearer, and what the audience must remember after hearing the piece once.
</Tip>
