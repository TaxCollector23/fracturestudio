title: "Academic Research Papers"
description: "Check the claims and citations in longer academic work."
---

For research papers, Fracture combines reasoning analysis with a source check so you can see both whether your argument holds and whether your evidence supports it.

## How to use it

- Run the paper in **Essay** mode for analytical and academic prose, or **Argument** mode if the paper is making a strong central claim it must defend.
- Use **Extreme** depth for the most thorough read on a longer piece.
- Review the source verification section to see which factual claims have public-web support and which need a stronger or more precise citation.

## What to look for

- Claims that need a source but do not name one
- Vague source references such as "studies show" or "experts say"
- Citations that are real but do not actually prove the attached conclusion
- Missing warrants between your evidence and your claims

<Note>
Fracture can produce a starter Works Cited or References list in MLA or APA from the sources it confirms, but you should open each source and confirm the exact passage before final submission.
</Note>
