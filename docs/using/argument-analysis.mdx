Argument mode is the default. Use it for debate cases, policy briefs, persuasive claims, opinion writing, and anything where you are trying to prove a statement with logic.

## How to run it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Argument mode">
    In the Studio, set the draft type to Argument.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a depth">
    Surface for a quick check, Medium for a full audit, or Extreme for the deepest read.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste and run">
    Paste your case and run Fracture. The report streams in as it is built.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the report grades

- **Claim, warrant, evidence, impact** — whether each claim is supported and connected to your thesis.
- **Burden of proof** — what you must prove for your conclusion to survive.
- **Collapse point** — the single load-bearing claim, definition, or causal link that most endangers the argument if defeated.
- **Dependency graph** — how your claims, warrants, and impacts depend on one another.
- **Attack tree** — the strongest distinct ways a skilled opponent could break your reasoning, with responses and crossfire questions.

<Tip>
After the audit, open the rebuttal builder to turn your own case into opponent preparation, or ask Fracture Chat about any specific pressure point.
</Tip>
